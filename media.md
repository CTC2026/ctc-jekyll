The Chinese Theater Collaborative presents modules organized by media type, in addition to the play-centered modules available under [Resources for Plays]({{ '/resources/' | relative_url }}). Each media type page introduces the format, its history in relation to traditional Chinese theater, and links to the relevant modules.

## Media Types

- [Recordings of Live Performances]({{ '/media/recorded-performances/' | relative_url }}) — Documented stage performances capturing traditional theatrical arts in their performance context.

- [Opera Film]({{ '/media/opera-film/' | relative_url }}) — Cinematic adaptations and studio productions blending traditional operatic performance with film technique.

- [Other Types of Film]({{ '/media/other-film/' | relative_url }}) — Silent films, live-action narrative films, and animated features that rework traditional Chinese plays.

- [Lianhuanhua (Narrative Comics)]({{ '/media/lianhuanhua/' | relative_url }}) — Sequential visual storytelling through Chinese comic booklets, from early Shanghai print culture to the socialist era.

- [Television]({{ '/media/television/' | relative_url }}) — Broadcast and streaming adaptations of classical plays, from early PRC programming to contemporary streaming drama.

- [Modern Theater and Mixed Media]({{ '/media/modern-theater/' | relative_url }}) — Contemporary theatrical works incorporating spoken drama, experimental staging, and cross-cultural performance.

- [Crowdsourced Student and Amateur Productions]({{ '/media/crowdsourced/' | relative_url }}) — Community-contributed theatrical materials from educational and non-professional sources.

## Opera Style

- [Kun Opera]({{ '/media/kun-opera/' | relative_url }}) — The oldest surviving form of Chinese musical theater, its revival, contemporary performance landscape, and fan communities.
