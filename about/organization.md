## Site Structure

The Chinese Theater Collaborative organizes content around iconic plays featured in two companion volumes: [*How To Read Chinese Drama in Chinese: A Language Companion*](http://cup.columbia.edu/book/how-to-read-chinese-drama-in-chinese/9780231209571) and [*How To Read Chinese Drama: A Guided Anthology*](http://cup.columbia.edu/book/how-to-read-chinese-drama/9780231546669). The site tracks these plays across various media including theater, film, and print.

Each play includes an introductory page with "General Background" information, followed by separate modules for different productions arranged chronologically. The platform provides text, images, and some video clips for each adaptation, using both traditional and simplified Chinese characters based on source materials.

The site acknowledges that dramatic texts are inherently malleable, noting that "virtually all later renditions of these plays take the form of adaptations." Production changes depend on factors like producers, audiences, and historical context, while different media shapes aesthetic form and social significance.

## Our Approach

This educational nonprofit employs selective topical analysis for each adaptation. Content typically includes introductory background on the production or medium type, followed by plot summaries and focused analysis of specific aesthetic tools the medium uses to communicate updated interpretations. Analysis is supported through images and video clips where available.
